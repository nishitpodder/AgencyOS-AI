# Risk Analysis Prompt

---

# Objective

Analyze the client's project requirements and identify all potential risks before generating a proposal.

The AI should help agencies avoid project failure, budget overruns, scope creep, and poor client communication.

---

# Purpose

The Risk Analysis module evaluates the project from a business perspective and highlights issues that may impact project success.

---

# Input

The AI will receive:

- Client Name
- Company Name
- Industry
- Business Type
- Target Audience
- Project Requirements
- Required Features
- Budget
- Timeline
- AI Lead Analysis
- Lead Score
- Additional Notes

---

# Risk Categories

## 1. Requirement Risk

Check:

- Missing Features
- Unclear Scope
- Incomplete Information
- Undefined Business Goals

---

## 2. Budget Risk

Evaluate whether:

- Budget is realistic
- Budget matches project scope
- Budget allows for quality delivery

---

## 3. Timeline Risk

Evaluate:

- Delivery deadline
- Resource availability
- Project complexity

Classify:

- Low
- Medium
- High

---

## 4. Technical Risk

Identify:

- Third-party integrations
- Payment gateways
- AI functionality
- Security requirements
- Performance concerns

---

## 5. Communication Risk

Evaluate:

- Requirement clarity
- Client responsiveness
- Decision-maker identified
- Frequency of communication

---

## 6. Business Risk

Evaluate:

- Long-term business value
- Client stability
- Repeat business potential
- Portfolio value

---

# Overall Risk Level

Assign one:

- Low Risk 🟢
- Medium Risk 🟡
- High Risk 🔴

---

# Recommendations

Provide practical recommendations such as:

- Gather More Information
- Revise Budget
- Extend Timeline
- Clarify Scope
- Schedule Discovery Meeting
- Proceed with Proposal

---

# Output Format

## Executive Summary

## Requirement Risks

## Budget Risks

## Timeline Risks

## Technical Risks

## Communication Risks

## Business Risks

## Overall Risk Level

## Recommended Next Steps

## Confidence Level (%)

---

# Rules

- Never invent project details.
- Clearly identify missing information.
- Explain why each risk matters.
- Suggest actions to reduce the risk.
- Keep recommendations practical and business-focused.
